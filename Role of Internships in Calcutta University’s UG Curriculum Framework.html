Role of Internships in Calcutta University’s UG Curriculum Framework

Integration of Internships in the New Curriculum

Under the latest Undergraduate Curriculum and Credit Framework (UG CCF) implemented by the University of Calcutta (aligned with NEP 2020), internships have been embedded as a formal part of the curriculum. Specifically, the framework introduces a Summer Internship component for undergraduate students. All UG students are expected to undertake one Summer Internship worth 3 credits during their course of study caluniv.ac.in. This internship is typically completed at the end of either the 2nd, 4th, or 6th semester, aligning with the multiple exit points of the 4-year UG program (after 1 year, 2 years, or 3 years respectively) caluniv.ac.in. In other words, the internship is built into the degree structure, ensuring students gain practical or field experience as part of their academic journey. It is not merely an extracurricular activity, but a credit-bearing requirement in the new curriculum framework.

Academic Credit and Evaluation for Internships

Yes – students receive academic credit and are formally evaluated (with marks) for the internship. The Summer Internship carries 3 credits, which count toward the total credits required for the degree caluniv.ac.in. This means the internship has weight in the academic credit system similar to a coursework module. Key details of how the internship is structured and assessed include:

  • Duration: The internship (or equivalent field project) spans about 15 days, typically scheduled during the summer recess (approximately May 16–30 each year) caluniv.ac.in.
  • Credit Allocation: The 3 credits are split between a project component and an evaluation component. Students must maintain a project notebook or report during the internship, which is assessed for 2 credits, and then undergo a viva-voce (oral examination) about their internship work, carrying the remaining 1 credit caluniv.ac.in.
  • Evaluation and Marks: The internship is graded. An external examiner (appointed by the university) evaluates the student’s project notebook and performance in the viva caluniv.ac.in. Students are required to achieve at least a minimum passing mark (e.g., 30% of the total marks) in this evaluation to earn the credits caluniv.ac.in.

Because the internship carries credits and is evaluated, students do receive formal recognition on their transcripts for completing it, just as they would for an academic course. The total credit requirement for the degree includes these 3 internship credits (for example, a 4-year Honours degree might total 172 credits, of which 3 are from the internship caluniv.ac.in).

Compulsory vs. Optional Nature of the Internship

In the current curriculum framework, the internship is not an optional add-on but rather an integral (compulsory) part of the undergraduate program. The phrasing in official documents states “all the students are required to do one 3 credits Summer Internship” during their UG studies caluniv.ac.in. Students have flexibility in when they complete this internship (depending on their academic progress or exit plans), but by the end of the 6th semester it must be completed by those continuing to the full degree caluniv.ac.in. This is particularly important because the university allows multiple exit points (certificate after 1 year, diploma after 2 years, or a 3-year degree), and any student opting for an early exit is required to have completed the internship and passed its evaluation before leaving caluniv.ac.in.

Even for students who do not exit early (i.e., continue to the full 3 or 4-year program), the internship remains a requirement within the curriculum. In practice, this means the internship is effectively compulsory for all students under the new credit framework – it’s a part of the credit requirements needed for graduation or for obtaining intermediate credentials. There isn’t an alternative non-internship route to those credits mentioned in the framework; however, the nature of the internship can be broad (it may include traditional internships, community engagement projects, or field-based projects as per the guidelines) caluniv.ac.in, giving some flexibility in implementation.

In summary: Under Calcutta University’s latest UG Curriculum & Credit Framework, internships carry formal academic weight. Students earn 3 academic credits for completing the mandated Summer Internship, and their work is assessed and graded (with a minimum marks requirement to pass) caluniv.ac.in. The internship forms a structured part of the curriculum rather than a purely optional experience, ensuring that even if it wasn’t previously compulsory, it now is an integrated credit-bearing component in the updated framework. This integration reflects the university’s adoption of NEP 2020 guidelines to blend practical experience with academic learning, rewarding students with both academic credit and evaluative marks for their internship work in the undergraduate program caluniv.ac.in.
